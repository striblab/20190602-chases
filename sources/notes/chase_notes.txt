Out of 465 vehicular pursuits by police spanning January 2016 to January 2019:

Nearly half of all chases begin with a traffic stop. A third start due to a stolen vehicle.

About 28 percent of pursuits end due to an unintentional crash, while another 23 percent end because the individual flees on foot.

Only about 15 percent of chases end because the driver stops voluntarily, while another 15 percent end due to supervisory direction.

Less than 10 percent of vehicle pursuits end in an injury.

About 77 percent span two miles or less.
